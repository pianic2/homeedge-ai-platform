# IHAP-45 — Environmental Sensor Comparative Evidence

**Issue:** [IHAP-45](https://niccolopiazzi01.atlassian.net/browse/IHAP-45)  
**Evidence status:** physical qualification in progress  
**Validation harness:** [`tools/hardware-validation/ihap-45-environmental-sensors/`](../../../tools/hardware-validation/ihap-45-environmental-sensors/)

This directory is the durable, reviewable evidence index for the owned DHT11, DHT22 and BME280 comparative qualification.

## Publication boundary

Raw acquisition data remain local to the Project Owner under the ignored harness directory:

```text
tools/hardware-validation/ihap-45-environmental-sensors/runs/
```

The repository must not contain:

- `serial.log`;
- `samples.jsonl`;
- phase-marker or reference-observation JSONL streams;
- serial-port histories or workstation paths;
- per-sample CSV exports;
- interactive HTML reports embedding the sample stream;
- unsanitized photographs or terminal screenshots.

Only allow-listed, aggregate summaries may be committed. This preserves transparency about method, pass/fail criteria, observed aggregate performance and decision boundaries without publishing reproducible raw telemetry.

The local raw runs remain the reproducible source used by the host tools. They are not the canonical repository artefact and are not required for an independent reviewer to understand what was tested, how it was evaluated or which claims remain unsupported.

## Current supported claims

The evidence observed so far supports these specimen-specific claims:

- the ESP32-C3 validation firmware builds, flashes and produces structured temperature/humidity records;
- the owned purple breakout responds at I2C address `0x76`;
- its chip-ID register returns `0x60`;
- it provides the BME280 humidity channel and is therefore qualified as the owned `BME280-OWNED-01` specimen;
- the owned DHT11, DHT22 and BME280 produce valid samples concurrently at 3.3 V in the tested wiring;
- pressure is excluded from the firmware schema and host validation;
- the USB-resilient collector captures startup metadata after ESP32-C3 native-USB re-enumeration;
- an initial integrated preflight detected a real brownout and correctly failed rather than suppressing the reset;
- the Project Owner subsequently reported that the staged functional-stability follow-up trials passed, including the gate needed to proceed to the controlled environmental run.

Machine-generated sanitized summaries for the completed staged stability trials remain pending local export.

## Claims still unsupported

The following remain `[UNVALIDATED]` until the 115-minute run is completed, validated, analyzed and summarized:

- absolute accuracy without an independent reference;
- comparative response time across controlled transitions;
- hysteresis and recovery behavior;
- repeatability across all required plateaus;
- final reference and fallback selection;
- final enclosure and placement fitness;
- quantitative current draw, rail voltage, regulator capacity or autonomy;
- behavior representative of every DHT11, DHT22 or BME280 unit;
- production, medical, precision, safety or certification maturity.

## Review rule

A summary is admissible only when:

1. it was generated from a local run by the repository-owned publisher;
2. it contains no raw sample or serial-log content;
3. its validation status and warnings are preserved rather than rewritten;
4. failed and partial runs are labelled as such;
5. the method and limitations remain visible;
6. the Project Owner reviews the summary before commit.
